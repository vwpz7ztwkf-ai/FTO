Model radio call-out
Order
84-26 → traffic stop → exact/current location → Indiana plate → phonetics.
Accuracy
Letters get WPD words. Digits are spoken one at a time: “four-seven-two,” not “four seventy-two.”
Safety
Do not put yourself behind the vehicle just to read a plate. Update dispatch when you can safely see it.

Keyboard: space reveal · enter new drill. Use the stop’s final location, not where you first noticed the violation.

Clean initial stop
84-26, traffic stop, [location], Indiana plate, [plate one character at a time].
Example: 84-26, traffic stop, eastbound State Road 32 at Union Street, Indiana plate, Adam-Boy-Charles-one-two-three.
No plate yet
84-26, traffic stop, [location], standby for plate.
Use this when traffic, angle, darkness, weather, or vehicle position keeps you from reading it safely.
Correcting a character
Correction on plate: third character is Ocean, not zero.
Say exactly what changed. Do not rush the correction.
Updated final location
84-26, updated stop location, [new location].
Use this when the vehicle rolls farther than the first location you called out.
Partial plate
84-26, traffic stop, [location], partial Indiana plate, [known characters], standby for the rest.
Call what you know; fill in the rest after the vehicle stops and it is safe.
